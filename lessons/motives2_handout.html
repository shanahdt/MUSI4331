Single Interval Motives¶

We saw an example of single-interval motives last week when we lookedat Debussy’s ‘Des pas sur la neige.’

This is an excellent way to generate renewed melodic and harmonic material throughout the piece. A single, salient interval is quite versatile, and can be used in a wide array of musical contexts.

For example. the fugue theme of Beethoven’s Piano Sonata in A-flat major (op. 110) is a single interval.
